HIPAA Compliance Statement

This document describes CodeMed Group's approach to HIPAA compliance, the data we handle, how we protect it, and what is required from customers who may handle Protected Health Information (PHI) in connection with our platform.

Platform Classification

CodeMed Group is a medical billing intelligence and HCC risk adjustment platform. The platform is designed to answer regulatory and coding questions — it is not an EHR, a clinical documentation system, or a claims clearinghouse. In its default configuration, the platform does not receive, store, or process PHI.

Under HIPAA, CodeMed Group may qualify as a Business Associate when a Covered Entity or another Business Associate uses the platform in a manner that involves PHI — for example, by submitting patient-specific diagnosis codes or member identifiers in API queries. In such cases, a BAA is required before use.

What Constitutes PHI on This Platform

The following would constitute PHI if submitted through our platform and should only be sent under a signed BAA:

  • Patient names, initials, or other direct identifiers
  • Medicare Beneficiary Identifiers (MBI) or Health Insurance Claim Numbers (HICN)
  • Dates of birth, admission dates, discharge dates linked to individuals
  • Diagnosis codes (ICD-10) tied to a specific, identifiable patient
  • Member IDs, Claim IDs, or other payer-assigned identifiers linked to individuals
  • Geographic data smaller than state level linked to individuals

Coding questions without patient identifiers are not PHI. For example, "Does F32.9 qualify for V28 HCC mapping?" is not PHI. "Patient John Doe, DOB 1942-03-15, MBI 1EG4-TE5-MK72, has F32.9 — does this map to V28 HCC?" contains PHI and requires a BAA.

Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption in transit: All connections use HTTPS with TLS 1.2 or higher. HTTP requests are redirected to HTTPS.
  • Encryption at rest: Data stored in SQLite databases on Railway infrastructure is encrypted at the filesystem level.
  • Access controls: API keys are hashed (SHA-256) before storage. Passwords are hashed using bcrypt. Session tokens are server-side and expire on logout.
  • Audit logging: All API requests, including query text, API key identifier, IP address, timestamp, and response latency, are logged in the audit_log table for up to 90 days.
  • Minimum necessary: The platform does not request or require PHI for its core functions. Users are instructed not to submit PHI without a BAA.

Subprocessors and Third-Party Data Sharing

Query text submitted to the CodeMed Group platform may be forwarded to the following subprocessors to generate AI responses:

  • Anthropic PBC: Query text is sent to Anthropic's Claude API to generate coding and compliance guidance. Anthropic's data processing terms govern their handling. Under default API terms, Anthropic does not train on API inputs. For customers requiring formal data processing agreements with Anthropic, please contact us.
  • Railway (Infrastructure): Application hosting and database storage. Data residency: United States.

Administrative Safeguards

  • Workforce training on PHI handling and HIPAA obligations
  • Designated Privacy Officer responsible for compliance oversight
  • Incident response procedures for potential PHI breaches
  • Vendor assessment process for evaluating subprocessor security posture
  • Annual review of this compliance statement and technical controls

Breach Notification

In the event of a breach involving PHI processed under a BAA, CodeMed Group will notify the affected Covered Entity within 60 days of discovery, as required under 45 CFR §164.410. Notification will include the nature of the PHI involved, the persons affected, the steps taken to mitigate harm, and contact information for further inquiry.

Your Responsibilities

As a customer, you are responsible for:

  • Ensuring you have a signed BAA with CodeMed Group before submitting any PHI
  • Training your staff not to submit PHI through the Demo or Starter tiers
  • Maintaining the confidentiality of your API keys and login credentials
  • Promptly reporting suspected unauthorized access to security@codemedgroup.com
  • Complying with your own HIPAA obligations as a Covered Entity or Business Associate
